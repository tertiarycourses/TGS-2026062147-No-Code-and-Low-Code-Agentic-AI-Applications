Frequently asked questions

Ava answers from exactly these six topics — and says so when a question falls outside them.

How do I order a refill, and how long does it take?

Give us your prescription number and the last four characters of your NRIC. Your order is prepared within 1 working day once the pharmacist has checked it. We refill repeat prescriptions only — a new prescription must come from your doctor.

How long does delivery take, and what does it cost?

Standard delivery is 2 to 3 working days, islandwide in Singapore. Delivery is free above $60; below that it is a flat $5. Refrigerated medicines are sent in a cold pack and must be handed to a person — they are never left at the door.

How long is a repeat prescription valid?

A repeat prescription is valid for 6 months or 3 refills, whichever comes first. After that your doctor must review it before we can dispense again. Controlled medicines cannot be refilled over the phone and must be collected in person.

Which payment methods do you accept?

PayNow, Visa, Mastercard, Apple Pay and Google Pay. We do not accept cash on delivery. We do not process insurance or Medisave claims, but we issue a receipt you can claim with.

When can I collect, and what do I bring?

The pharmacy is open Monday to Saturday, 9am to 7pm, closed Sunday. A prepared order is held for 7 days, then returned to stock. Bring your order number and photo identification.

Can Ava tell me how to take my medicine?

No — and this is deliberate. Ava will not advise on a dose, a side effect, a substitute or whether a medicine is safe for you. She offers a pharmacist callback instead, within one working day. A confident wrong answer from a pharmacy is not an inconvenience; it is a safety incident.

How the voice agent works

1Browser

The page starts a WebRTC call with the Vapi Web SDK using your public key.

2Vapi

Vapi runs speech-to-text and the voice, then calls your n8n workflow as its Custom LLM.

3Guardrail

The agent answers only from the FAQ, and refuses every request for medical advice.

A public key is designed to be visible in the browser and can only start calls. Your private key manages assistants and must never appear in front-end code.
